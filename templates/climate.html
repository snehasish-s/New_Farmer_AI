Frequently Asked Questions About Climate Change

What is climate change?
Climate change refers to significant, long-term changes in the patterns of temperature, precipitation, wind, and other aspects of the Earth's climate system. These changes are primarily driven by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere.
The Earth's climate has changed throughout history, but the current warming trend is of particular significance because it is predominantly the result of human activities since the mid-20th century and proceeding at a rate that is unprecedented over millennia.
How does climate change affect agriculture?
Climate change affects agriculture through changes in average temperatures, rainfall patterns, extreme weather events, and the spread of pests and diseases. These changes can reduce crop yields, affect soil health, and threaten food security in vulnerable regions.
Changes in climate can lead to reduced nutritional quality of crops, decreased water availability for irrigation, and increased heat stress on livestock. Adaptation strategies include developing climate-resilient crops, improving water management, and adopting sustainable farming practices.
What is NDVI and how does it relate to crop health?
NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) is a measure of the health and density of vegetation. It uses satellite imagery to analyze light reflection - healthy plants reflect more near-infrared light and less visible light. Higher NDVI values indicate healthier, more dense vegetation.
NDVI values range from -1 to +1. Values close to zero or negative typically indicate water, snow, or bare soil, while values above 0.5 generally indicate healthy vegetation. Farmers and researchers use NDVI to monitor crop health, estimate yields, and identify problem areas in fields.

What can farmers do to adapt to climate change?
Farmers can adopt various climate-smart agricultural practices including: diversifying crops, implementing water conservation techniques, using cover crops to improve soil health, adopting integrated pest management, and utilizing climate-resilient crop varieties.
Other adaptation strategies include adjusting planting dates to align with changing seasonal patterns, investing in weather insurance, using precision agriculture technologies to optimize resource use, and participating in carbon sequestration programs that can provide additional income streams.
How does temperature increase affect plant growth?
Higher temperatures can accelerate plant development, reducing the growing period and potentially decreasing yields. Extreme heat can cause heat stress, reduce pollination success, and increase water requirements. Each crop has an optimal temperature range for growth.
